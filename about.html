ABOUT CELINE

Celine Marie Claudette Dion was born on March 30, 1968, in Charlemagne, Quebec, Canada. They later opened a piano bar, where the 5-year-old Dion would perform to the delight of customers. At the age of 12, Dion recorded a demo tape of a song she had written with her mother.

A star from a young age, singer Celine Dion had recorded nine French albums and won numerous awards by the time she was 18. She recorded her first English language album, Unison, in 1990.

Celine Dion’s real breakthrough into pop music stardom came in 1992 when recorded the theme to Disney’s hit animated feature Beauty and the Beast. She went on to record several hits, including: “The Power of Love,” “Because You Loved Me,” “My Heart Will Go On” and “I’m Your Angel.” She has become a legend of popular music, one of the most powerful and moving voices ever heard.

After over 35 years as a performing and recording artist, Celine Dion has become a legend of popular music, one of the most powerful and moving voices ever heard. An honest woman who sings from the heart. It’s as simple and true that as Celine.
